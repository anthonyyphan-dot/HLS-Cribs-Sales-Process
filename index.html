Introduction

Open with clarity, uncover the why, explain the value proposition, and handle early resistance with AAPA before the call loses momentum.

Intro + AAPA
Opening script

Default introduction

“Hi this is XYZ calling from Aussie Home Loans, I’m one of the mortgage brokers here and I’m just following up on a bit of research you’ve done on our platform recently. I understand you’re looking to review your rate or purchase a property, how’s that going so far?”
Purchase

Uncover the motivation

“That’s exciting, is this your first property? Awesome, what’s motivating you to start looking now — are you sick of paying rent, or wanting more independence?”
Refinance

Go beyond rate-shopping

“That’s all good everyone always wants a cheaper rate, but were you saving up towards buying something else or using that extra money towards paying down the home loan sooner?”
Value proposition

Purchase value prop

“As a mortgage broker my job is to review your finances alongside the 25 different lenders that we work with and recommend the right bank for you. We also have inhouse buyers agents and conveyancers, so we’re a one stop shop for buying property.”
Refi value prop

Refinance value prop

“We work with 25 different lenders so I’ll be able to quickly review your rate and identify what your top 3 options would look like without you having to speak to any of those lenders.”
Agenda

Set expectations

“What I’ll do now is confirm some of the details that we have here on the platform to make sure everything is accurate and by the end of the call I’ll be able to let you know your borrowing capacity and send through your top 3 options.”
AAPA framework Acknowledge, Agree, Provide Information, Agenda. Use it when the customer pushes back early so you can keep the call moving without sounding combative.

I can definitely give you a call back at another time but I just wanted to make sure we could help you out and make sure it was worth your time — were you just looking at refinancing your property or purchasing a property?”

I’ve got another mortgage broker

“That’s great to hear you’ve got another broker doing some work for you. I’m not looking to step on anyone’s toes right now. What we can actually do is provide you with a few more options from our panel of lenders so you can make sure you definitely proceed with the right option — what has your broker offered you so far?”

I think I’m all good for now

“That’s all good, I just wanted to give you a quick call since you came onto our platform recently to do some research. Just wanted to double check and make sure you’re all good as well. What was the rate you are sitting on at the moment so I can tell you if we can do anything better?”

I was just doing some research

“That’s all good, I’m glad you were able to use our platform to do a bit of research. I just wanted to reach out and see if I could assist with anything off the back of that. When were you looking to potentially purchase a property?”

Fact Find

On the initial call, grab only the essential information so you can keep momentum and progress quickly.

Keep it essential

Essential information checklist

Call cue

Do not over-collect on the first call. The goal is enough detail to identify borrowing position, compare lenders properly, and move to the next step.

Keep the pace Confirm details cleanly, avoid getting lost in edge cases, and keep reminding the customer what outcome you are working toward.

Providing Solution / Product Comparison

Move from options to recommendation. This section is built to keep the customer engaged while you compare lenders live.

Guide them live

Before sending comparison

  • Complete the Needs and Objectives tab first.
  • Understand all customer preferences before presenting products.
  • Select a minimum of 3 products.
  • Include the cheapest-rate product first, even if it is not your recommended lender.

While presenting live

  • Send the comparison link live and wait for the customer to open it.
  • Guide them through the login process.
  • Talk through rate, repayment, and fees for each option.
  • Keep checking engagement with questions they can answer on-screen.

Engagement prompts

  • “What rate can you see?”
  • “Have you banked with XYZ before?”
  • “Can you see what the repayment is?”

Finish with a recommendation

After showing each product, make a clear recommendation on which lender is best and explain why the other two are not the strongest fit.

6 Steps of Closing

Close by getting a lender preference, selling the logic and emotion, locking commitment, then working through the common objections that show up right before action.

Commitment + rebuttals

1. Pick a bank

After recommending a lender, ask which bank they prefer and ensure they tentatively select one even if they raise an objection. Use AAPA if objection arises.

2. Sell intellectually

Re-explain the savings. Use the savings calculator where relevant. For purchases, show how extra repayments, offset, or redraw can improve the picture.

3. Sell emotionally

Link back to their why and build urgency and excitement — the sooner they get pre-approved, the sooner they can make an offer or stop paying someone else’s mortgage.

4. Gain a commitment

“So just want to confirm, you’re happy with XYZ Bank which we have presented. If you change your mind we can always change to another lender.”

5. VVDR

VOI, Valuation, Documents, Referrals.

6. Close with an agreed outcome

Ensure BBAs are booked within 48 hours and tailor the ask based on whether the customer is low intent or high intent.

Low intent customer prompt

“If you wish to proceed with XYZ bank, I’ll need you to upload your documents before our next appointment so I can review the servicing and prepare the application.”

High intent customer prompt

“Here at Aussie we actually offer a same day submission within 24 hours, would you be able to quickly jump in a video call with me now and we can finalise the details and get the application underway now?”
Common objections during the close Use these to keep the customer moving toward a lender preference and a concrete next step, rather than letting the call end in a soft maybe.

I’ll chat to my partner first

“I completely understand, not looking to proceed without your partner’s approval of course and we would have to sit down all together to do a video call before proceeding anyway, so I can set up what the next steps would look like for you both. Does XYZ seem like the lender you are leaning towards right now?”

Let me think about it and I’ll get back to you

“No problems at all, we aren’t looking to proceed right now since I will need your documents so you’ll have some time to have a think. However, so I can show you what those documents look like, does one of the lenders we spoke about stand out more than the other?”

Don’t think I’ll do anything right now

“No stress, I know I just went through a lot of information so it’s probably a lot to take in and make a decision on the spot. However, was there anything specific holding you back from proceeding, as I can see we can definitely move forward with the refi/AIP for you quickly?”

Can you push it back for 1 month

“Completely understand, I know you are probably busy and have a lot on your plate right now. However, that’s where we can assist and manage this process for you from start to finish. We could possibly even have it approved and settled within a month — all you’d have to do now is upload your ID and payslips and we’ll be able to submit your application. Would you be free now or this afternoon to complete a quick video call?”

I’m going to wait and save up a bit more

“Completely understand, timing the market can sometimes help. However, what is holding you back from purchasing at the moment, as I have run the numbers and can see you would be able to enter the market right now and we could get you sorted with a pre-approval in your back pocket if you did decide to make an offer?”

Alternative pathways if saving more is the blocker

  • If deposit is low: offer guarantor, gift, FHGS, or similar support options.
  • If deposit is okay but target property is out of reach: explore a smaller property first and build equity.
  • If owner-occupied purchase is out of reach: test whether an investment property first could work and offer to run the numbers.

Follow Up

Follow up needs a purpose. Re-sell, link back to the why, push for document completion, and use the rate-rise objection scripts when customers stall.

Follow up + rate objections

Rules to follow

  • BBA should be booked within 48 hours.
  • Call a minimum of 3 times.
  • Space each attempt 1–2 days apart.
  • Each follow up should re-sell and reconnect to the original reason they engaged.

Document chase script

“I can see there’s only a couple of things left. I can re-send the link now — can you please upload while I’m on the phone with you so we can get it out of the way?”

Attempt 1

Call at the time of the BBA. If no-show, leave a voice message.

Attempt 2

Call again. If no answer, leave a voice message and send an email reminder.

Attempt 3

Call again. If still no answer, leave a voice message advising the file will be closed off unless they re-engage.

Documents issue

  • Get the application to the bank ASAP to avoid the queue and get approval moving.
  • Link back to the why, build urgency, and get the customer excited.
  • Use a deadline: “If you can’t provide any documents to me by X date, I will close off your file and you can reach back out when you have more time.”

Intent issue

  • “As I have discussed there is definitely savings to be made, which part of the process or lender is holding you back?”
  • If the customer still has not made up their mind after 3 attempts, close lost.
